# Project Charter — EdTech Scholarship Retention

## Business context
A subscription-based EdTech platform launched a 50% scholarship campaign for the first 3 months to boost signups.

## Problem statement
Leadership observed strong signup growth during the campaign but suspects churn increased after the discount ended.

## Objectives
1) Measure whether the campaign cohort churns more than non-campaign cohorts.
2) Estimate LTV differences (discount vs non-discount).
3) Identify segments with high retention and high LTV.
4) Recommend whether to continue, modify, or stop the campaign.

## Stakeholders
- CEO (growth + sustainability)
- Head of Marketing (campaign performance)
- Finance (MRR stability, LTV)

## Scope
Included:
- Retention/churn analysis
- Cohorts (pre/during/post campaign)
- LTV estimates

Excluded (for now):
- Causal inference (A/B test not available)
- Full ML churn prediction (optional later)

## Success criteria
- Clear cohort comparisons
- Transparent definitions + assumptions
- Actionable recommendations tied to metrics

## Data Constraints Identified
- No explicit signup_date or cancellation_date available.
- Dataset represents a snapshot in time.
- Tenure field will be used as a proxy for subscription duration.
